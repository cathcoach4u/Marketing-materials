Section 1

The one thing you need to understand first

Google AI Overviews and Google Search are not separate systems. Google confirmed in May 2026: AI features run on the same core ranking and quality systems as standard search. There is no separate "AI optimisation" strategy. If your page ranks in the top 10, it is likely to be cited in AI Overviews. If it does not rank, it will not be cited.

76% of all URLs cited in AI Overviews also rank in Google's top 10 organic results. The median ranking position of the most-cited URL is position 2. This means the plan is simple: rank well, and the AI visibility follows.

What this means for Coach4U. The tactics that will get you cited when someone asks Google AI "where can I find an ADHD coach in Sydney" are the same tactics that will rank you organically. Original content, clear expertise, fast site, strong internal structure. No special files. No tricks. No separate AI strategy.

What Google explicitly says does NOT work: llms.txt files, special AI markup, chunking content into tiny pieces, rewriting copy in FAQ-only format, adding every possible keyword to schema, or creating pages specifically for AI search. These are myths. Google's May 2026 guide names them directly.

Section 3

Google Business Profile

Coach4U qualifies as a "service area business" (SAB). You can set up a GBP profile, hide your home address, and list service areas. The profile appears in Google Search and Maps for relevant searches in your chosen areas.

Profile type

Set up as a service area business. Enter your address for verification only — it will not appear publicly. List Northern Beaches, Greater Sydney, and online (Australia) as service areas.

Primary category

Use "Marriage counselor" as primary category (direct match for couples work). Add "Life coach" as secondary. Do not use Psychologist or Psychotherapist — these imply clinical registration you do not hold.

Key attributes to enable

  • Online appointments
  • Online services
  • Identifies as women-led (optional)

These confirm to Google — and to searchers — that you serve clients remotely.

Reviews are the biggest lever

For SABs without a physical address, reviews are the single most powerful ranking signal in the local pack. 10 high-quality Google reviews will outperform most other GBP optimisation work.

Honest limitation. Without a pinned physical address, Coach4U will not appear in the Google Maps local pack the same way a storefronted business does. For national reach, organic search via the website is a stronger channel than GBP. GBP is worth setting up for Sydney local search and to build review credibility — but do not expect it to drive national enquiries.

Section 4

WordPress technical SEO

Only 43.4% of WordPress sites on mobile pass all three Google Core Web Vitals. The fixes below address the most common failure points. Start with the hosting speed check — it is the most common root cause for WordPress sites on GoDaddy.

Install and configure

Rank Math (free)

Replace or install over Yoast. Rank Math is 2.5MB vs Yoast's 7MB, handles LocalBusiness schema, Service schema, sitemaps, and redirections at no cost. Do not run both plugins simultaneously.

Caching plugin

WP Rocket ($59/year) is the most effective paid option. W3 Total Cache is free. Avoid LiteSpeed Cache — it only works with LiteSpeed servers, and GoDaddy shared hosting uses Apache.

Image optimisation

Install ShortPixel or Imagify. These compress and convert all images to WebP automatically on upload. Do not lazy-load your LCP image (the main hero/header) — this is a common mistake that directly hurts Core Web Vitals.

Hosting speed check first

Run coach4u.com.au through PageSpeed Insights. If Time to First Byte (TTFB) is above 600ms, the problem is the server, not the plugins. Upgrading to GoDaddy Managed WordPress will fix this — plugins alone cannot.

Schema markup to add

LocalBusiness / ProfessionalService

Add to Homepage and Contact page via Rank Math. Captures practice name, phone, service area, hours. Critical for local search signals.

Service schema

Add to each individual service page — couples counselling, individual coaching, ADHD coaching, NDIS program. Rank Math handles this via its on-page settings.

Person schema

Add to the About page. Name, credentials, qualifications, social profiles. This is an E-E-A-T signal — it tells Google that a real expert with real credentials wrote the content.

FAQPage schema

Add to any page with a visible Q&A section. Generates rich results in Google. Only apply where the page content visibly matches — do not add schema for content that is not on the page.

Technical fixes

✓

Google Search Console: Set up and submit sitemap (Rank Math generates it at yourdomain.com.au/sitemap.xml). This is how Google discovers new and updated content.

✓

Permalink structure: Settings > Permalinks > Post Name. URLs should read /couples-counselling, not /?p=123. Set once and do not change.

✓

Noindex thin pages: Apply noindex to tag archives, date archives, author archives, and WordPress search result pages. These generate duplicate thin content that dilutes the site.

✓

HTTPS forced site-wide: Verify every URL redirects to HTTPS. Mixed content (some pages HTTP, some HTTPS) creates ranking issues.

✓

Image alt text: Fill in descriptive alt text for every image. This is an accessibility requirement and an SEO signal — especially for a practice where the About photo and session environment matter to searchers.

✓

Author bio with credentials: Every published page should show Cath's name and qualifications. Google's E-E-A-T framework rewards demonstrated real-world expertise. For health and wellbeing content this matters more than most niches.

Section 5

Content structure for Google

Google rewards sites that clearly own a topic area. The pillar page approach — one comprehensive page per core topic, supported by shorter posts answering sub-questions — is the most reliable way to build authority over time.

Pillar 1: ADHD Coaching

  • Pillar page: What is ADHD coaching and how does it work?
  • Supporting: ADHD in adults vs children
  • Supporting: ADHD and relationships
  • Supporting: How ThriveHQ works
  • Supporting: NDIS funding for ADHD coaching

Pillar 2: Couples Counselling

  • Pillar page: Couples counselling Sydney — what to expect
  • Supporting: EFT vs Gottman — what is the difference?
  • Supporting: When is couples counselling useful?
  • Supporting: How Imago therapy works
  • Supporting: Relationships and attachment styles

Pillar 3: Strengths Coaching

  • Pillar page: CliftonStrengths coaching — how it works
  • Supporting: What are your top 5 strengths?
  • Supporting: Strengths and ADHD
  • Supporting: Using strengths in relationships
  • Supporting: Strengths for leaders and teams

Why this structure works for AI Overviews. 44% of AI citations come from the first 30% of a page. A pillar page that opens with a clear, direct answer to the main question — and then expands — is exactly the format that earns citations. Write the answer in the opening paragraph, not after a preamble. Then use H2/H3 headings for the rest.

Content signals that get cited in AI Overviews

First-hand expertise

Google explicitly rewards content based on "unique perspective from personal experience." Your frameworks (ThriveHQ Brain Pulse, SAFEty Pulse, Name-Claim-Aim) are original. Write about how they work in practice — not in generic advice language. This is exactly the non-commodity content Google cannot source elsewhere.

Original data and outcomes

Content with original statistics is cited 30–40% more in AI responses. You do not need a clinical trial — even anonymised observations from your practice ("most members report X within the first 4 weeks") count as original data. Use them.

Fresh content

85% of AIO citations come from content published within the last two years. For Coach4U, this means publishing new content regularly (not just editing existing pages) and refreshing high-value pages at least once per year.

LinkedIn and Instagram as citation sources

LinkedIn is the second most-cited domain in AI Overviews, and the first for professional service queries. Instagram now processes roughly 6.5 billion searches per day. Optimise your Instagram bio with location, specialisation, and a clear call to action. Both platforms feed AI citations directly. Use /social to publish weekly.

Australian directory listings

Listing consistently across health and coaching directories builds "citations" — references to Coach4U's name, address, and phone number across the web. NAP consistency (identical name, address, and phone format across every listing) is a local SEO ranking factor. These directories are worth prioritising:

✓

HealthShare and HotDoc — primary health and allied health directories in Australia. Set up a profile on both.

✓

Health Engine — widely used for allied health bookings. A listing here builds citation authority even if bookings come through your own site.

✓

PACFA or ACA directory — list under your relevant professional membership. These are trusted domains in Google's eyes for counselling searches.

✓

Gallup-certified coach directory — for CliftonStrengths coaching, the Gallup partner or certified coach directory is a high-authority niche citation.

✓

NAP consistency: use exactly the same business name spelling, phone format, and address wording across every listing. Inconsistencies confuse Google's local ranking system.

Section 6

What to do and what to avoid

Do

  • Write from first-hand experience with your frameworks
  • Put the key answer in the first paragraph of every page
  • Use clear H2/H3 headings throughout
  • Refresh key service pages at least once per year
  • Add FAQ sections to service pages with schema
  • Build pillar pages that own a topic fully
  • Get Google reviews from current and past clients
  • Publish to LinkedIn regularly — it feeds AI citations
  • Fix TTFB first if it is above 600ms
  • Add schema for Person, LocalBusiness, Service
  • Link related pages together internally

Do not

  • Create a separate AI writing strategy — it does not exist
  • Add llms.txt or special AI markup files
  • Chunk content into tiny pieces for AI
  • Rewrite in FAQ-only format across the whole site
  • Over-invest in schema trying to trigger AIO
  • Create pages for every keyword variation
  • Use the category "Psychologist" in GBP
  • Add LiteSpeed Cache on GoDaddy shared hosting
  • Apply lazy loading to the hero/header image (hurts LCP)
  • Leave author bio blank on published pages
  • Run Yoast and Rank Math simultaneously
