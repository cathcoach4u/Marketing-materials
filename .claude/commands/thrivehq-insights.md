## Report Structure

### 1. Themes This Week
2 to 4 main themes addressed in the session. One short paragraph each. Describe broadly and frame as coaching topics, not clinical issues.

### 2. Language Clients Used
8 to 12 paraphrased phrases capturing how participants described their experience in everyday terms. Authentic, de-identified, plain language — not clinical.

### 3. Proof Points
4 to 6 specific outcomes or shifts from the session. One sentence each. Anonymised evidence that the coaching is effective.

### 4. Content Angles
A table with 3 to 5 content ideas inspired by the session. Columns: Angle | Suggested format | Brief description.

### 5. Marketing Action This Week
One concrete, specific marketing action for the coming week. One paragraph. Name the format, the message, and why it will resonate with Coach4U's audience.

## Module-Specific Guidance

### Module 1: ThriveHQ (ADHD Group Coaching)
- Multiple members in a group. Focus on common ADHD-related struggles and wins in daily life.
- Tie themes to the four ThriveHQ anchors where relevant: Capacity, Wellbeing, Using My Strengths, Executive Functions.
- Frame challenges as issues of capacity or needing better systems — not personal failings.
- Capture distinctive ADHD-related language members use (paraphrased).
- Highlight community support, body doubling, accountability, and real-life strategies.
- Never suggest the group is treatment for a disorder. Emphasise working with the brain, not against it.

### Module 2: Individual Coaching
- Themes around personal development, clarity, momentum, decision-making, transitions, strengths use, and daily functioning.
- Note how the client describes their challenges or growth in their own words (paraphrased).
- Proof points: clearer goals, increased confidence, better self-management, positive mindset shifts.
- Highlight how coaching is tailored to the individual's strengths and values.
- No therapeutic framing. Keep focus on actionable growth and problem-solving.

### Module 3: Couples Coaching
- Themes of communication, understanding differences, trust, emotional safety, conflict resolution, and aligned goals.
- Capture how the couple describes their dynamic in everyday relational terms (paraphrased).
- Proof points: new communication techniques, positive connection, practical experiments being tried.
- Frame improvements in terms of better understanding and strengthened connection.
- No blame, no clinical terms. Describe issues neutrally and improvements constructively.

### Module 4: Business/Leadership Coaching
- Themes of leadership development, role clarity, team communication, strategic thinking, and performance.
- Paraphrase how the client or team describes workplace challenges or wins in plain business terms.
- Proof points: improved leadership capacity, team functioning, clarity, or skills built.
- Highlight how Coach4U's structured, strengths-focused approach produced tangible business improvement.
- No jargon or academic framing. Results-oriented, capability-building language only.
