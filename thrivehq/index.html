What is ThriveHQ

A 6-month ADHD coaching membership with twice-weekly live sessions, a private community, and ADHD-friendly tools designed to help you move from scattered to settled.

A space where you are accepted as you are and supported in a way that actually helps you thrive.

Tuesday
6:00 PM AEST  |  1.5 hours

A coaching-focused session where we look at the week ahead, work through challenges together, and build momentum. This is where you plan, reflect, and feel supported.

Thursday
12:00 PM AEST  |  1 hour

Action-focused body doubling. A space to work on your tasks alongside others. You are not alone. You are accountable. And you actually get things done.

The foundation you can count on

  • You do not have to arrive on time. Join when you can.
  • There is no homework. No pressure between sessions.
  • Life happens. You will miss sessions. That is okay. Just keep coming back.

ThriveHQ runs in 10-week blocks that align with the NSW school term rhythm. Every 10 weeks, there is a 2-week break. Over summer, there is a 6-week reset period. You can join at any time.

Why I created ThriveHQ

I am not observing ADHD from a distance. I live it. I know the exhaustion of feeling like you are constantly behind, the shame of another abandoned system, the loneliness of thinking you are the only one who cannot seem to get it together.

For years, I tried to force my brain into systems designed for neurotypical people. I kept failing and blaming myself. Then I discovered strengths-based coaching and everything changed. I stopped trying to fix what was "wrong" and started building scaffolding that worked with my brain, not against it.

That is why I created ThriveHQ. Not another course you will abandon. Not another productivity hack that makes you feel worse. A real community where you show up as you are, where missing a session does not mean you have failed, where someone actually understands when you say "I forgot" or "I got overwhelmed."

ThriveHQ has been running for nearly 5 years. People do not just join. They stay. They come back week after week because this is where they finally feel seen, supported, and capable of moving forward.

Cath
