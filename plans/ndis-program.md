## How NDIS Funding Works for This Program

NDIS participants receive funding allocated across categories. Parent training is funded under Capacity Building, not Core Supports. The funding sits in the participant's plan and is claimed by the provider session by session.

#### Funding category

Capacity Building → Improved Daily Living. This is for supports that build a participant's skills or, in this case, the skills of the parents and carers supporting them.

Support Category 15

#### Line item

Training for Parents/Carers. This line item explicitly funds training delivered to parents and carers of NDIS participants — not just to the participant directly.

15_038_0117_1_3

#### Hourly rate cap

The NDIS Price Guide sets a maximum hourly rate. As of 2024–25 this is approximately \$193.99 per hour for this line item. Providers cannot charge above this cap but can charge below it.

~\$193.99/hr cap

#### Who you can invoice

Without NDIS registration, you can invoice self-managed and plan-managed participants. To invoice NDIA-managed participants you need to be a registered NDIS provider. Most parents in your target market are plan-managed.

Self & plan-managed

#### Eligible participants

The child must be an NDIS participant with funding allocated to Training for Parents/Carers in their plan. Parents do not need their own NDIS plan. This is the child's funding, used to train the parent.

#### Group delivery

NDIS allows group delivery using a participant-to-provider ratio. For a 1:10 ratio, you can claim the hourly rate divided by the group size, plus separately claimable non-face-to-face time for preparation, materials, and reports.

## 10-Week Program Structure

Draft curriculum. Each week is 1.5 hours, live online via Microsoft Teams. The program builds in sequence — early weeks focus on understanding, middle weeks on skill-building, final weeks on application and integration.

Week 1

#### Foundations and welcome

Group agreements, intro to strengths-based parenting, what makes this approach different.

Week 2

#### Knowing your child

Spotting your child's natural strengths, energy patterns, and what calms or activates them.

Week 3

#### Knowing yourself

Your own capacity, regulation, and the Window of Tolerance. You can't pour from an empty cup.

Week 4

#### Communication that lands

Language and tone that works with neurodivergent brains. Co-regulation in everyday moments.

Week 5

#### Routines and scaffolding

Building structure that supports your child without becoming rigid. Good day plan, bad day plan.

Week 6

#### Meltdowns and recovery

What's happening in the nervous system. How to respond in the moment and repair afterwards.

Week 7

#### Strengths in conflict

When your strengths and your child's strengths clash. Reframing conflict as a strengths mismatch.

Week 8

#### Self-compassion as a parenting skill

Letting go of guilt, modelling self-kindness, and parenting from values not fear.

Week 9

#### Working with school and supports

Advocating for your child without burning out. Using NDIS reviews and school meetings well.

Week 10

#### Integration and what's next

Pulling it all together. Each parent leaves with a personalised plan. Optional follow-up support.

## Getting Paid for Your Time — Cancellation and No-Show Policy

This is the critical piece. You are committing 10 weeks of facilitation time and capping the group at 10 spots. When someone enrolls, that spot is unavailable to others. You need to be paid for it whether they attend or not.

#### NDIS cancellation rules work in your favour

The NDIS Price Guide allows providers to charge full rate for short-notice cancellations and no-shows, provided the policy is in the service agreement and the participant has agreed to it.

- **Less than 7 clear business days notice** for capacity building supports: 100% of the scheduled support fee is chargeable
- **No-show without notice:** full session fee chargeable
- **The 7-day rule** means once a cohort starts, any missed session is billable as long as the participant cannot give 7 business days notice (which is impossible for weekly sessions)
- **Maximum claims per year:** NDIS limits cancellations to 12 per participant per year, but for a 10-week program with 10 sessions you are well within that

### How to make this work in practice

Build the cancellation policy into your service agreement. Explain it upfront in plain language so parents understand from the start. Then enforce it. The structure that works is enrolment plus commitment, not pay-as-you-go.

#### Enrolment, not attendance

Parents enrol for the full 10-week program. Their NDIS plan is invoiced weekly for each scheduled session regardless of attendance. The spot is theirs whether they use it or not.

#### One advance week's notice

If a parent knows they will miss a session, they let you know the week before. This gives you the option to record a brief catch-up message or share notes. The session is still billed.

#### Replacement content for missed sessions

For each missed session, send a brief written summary or recording link. This is genuinely valuable and removes any feeling that the parent is paying for nothing.

#### Exit policy

If a parent needs to withdraw entirely, 2 weeks notice required. This protects you and the group dynamic, and aligns with NDIS service agreement best practice.


## Service Agreement Essentials

Every participant needs a signed service agreement before the program starts. NDIS requires it and it protects you. Use **/copywriting** to draft the agreement. Key clauses to include:

#### Scope of service

Name the program, the line item being claimed, the dates and times of sessions, the total program fee, and what is included (group sessions, materials, final report).

#### Cancellation policy

State the 7-business-day notice rule. State that sessions missed within that window are charged at full rate. Explain that this is consistent with NDIS Price Guide rules.

#### Privacy and group confidentiality

Group members agree to keep what is shared in group confidential. State your privacy practices and how participant information is stored and shared.

#### Goals and outcomes

Each participant sets 1–2 personal goals at the start of the program. These are documented in the agreement and referred to in the final report. Required for NDIS evidence.

#### Code of conduct

Brief statement of how participants are expected to engage in group sessions — respectful, on-time, cameras on where possible. Reserve the right to remove disruptive participants.

#### Plan manager details

Collect plan manager contact details and NDIS number at enrolment. You will invoice the plan manager weekly. Confirm funding availability for the full program upfront.

## Risks to Watch

Things that could go wrong. Plan for them in advance.

#### NDIS plan funding gaps

A parent commits but the child's plan doesn't have enough Training for Parents/Carers funding. Confirm funding availability before enrolment. Get plan manager confirmation in writing.

#### NDIS Price Guide changes

Rates and rules change annually on 1 July. Build a review point into your annual planning to update fees and the service agreement.

#### Plan manager payment delays

Some plan managers pay weekly, others monthly. Cash flow impact if you are running multiple cohorts. Build a 4–6 week cash buffer.

#### Group cohesion issues

A disruptive participant can break a group. Screen at intake — a 30-minute pre-program call lets you assess fit and brief expectations. Reserve the right to remove participants.

#### Low cohort numbers

First cohort may not fill. Decide your minimum viable number (e.g. 5) and what happens below that — delay start, run smaller, or refund deposits.

#### Scope creep into therapy

Some parents will want individual therapy support. Be clear: this is coaching and training, not therapy. Refer to your individual coaching service for deeper work. Keep the group on curriculum.
