# Coach4U Marketing Plan

## Positioning and Mission

**Coach4U** is a fully online, strengths-based coaching and counselling practice based in Sydney, Australia. The practice serves individuals, couples, adults with ADHD, and organisations — helping them move toward clarity, stronger connection, and sustainable growth.

Coach4U is not a psychology practice. It bridges the gap between traditional therapy and generic life coaching: more structure and practical tools than conventional counselling, and more psychological depth and evidence-based rigour than most coaching services.

### What makes Coach4U different

#### Coaching plus counselling, not one or the other

Evidence-based frameworks (EFT, Gottman, Imago, Narrative Therapy, TA) delivered in a forward-looking coaching style that focuses on action, goals, and practical tools — not diagnosis or clinical language.

#### Strengths first, problems second

Every service builds on what clients already do well. Challenges are reframed as opportunities to use strengths in a new way. This is fundamentally different from problem-focused therapy.

#### Proprietary frameworks that create structure

ThriveHQ (ADHD coaching), Relationship Compass (couples), SAFEty Pulse, Brain Pulse, and Strengths Development Pathway are all built in-house and cannot be found elsewhere.

#### ADHD and neurodiversity expertise

Deep specialisation informed by lived experience. ThriveHQ is built specifically for ADHD adults — addressing executive function, isolation, and start-stop cycles in ways generic programs cannot.

#### Continuous support between sessions

Daily check-ins, community, and digital tools mean clients aren't left alone between sessions. This level of between-session support is rare in both coaching and counselling practices.

#### Fully online, all of Australia

Delivered via Microsoft Teams. Accessible regardless of location, easy to fit around busy schedules, and practical for the ADHD clients who struggle with commuting and waiting rooms.


## Target Audiences

Four primary audiences, each with distinct problems and entry points into Coach4U's services.

### Adults with ADHD

Primary audience for ThriveHQ

"I've tried every system and nothing sticks. I know what I should be doing but I can't make myself do it."

**What we offer:** ThriveHQ ADHD group coaching membership — a structured community that works with the ADHD brain, not against it. Brain Pulse weekly tracking, peer support, and strengths-based scaffolding. Outcome: from chaos toward calm, focus, and self-compassion.

### Couples

Individual and joint sessions

"We keep having the same argument. We love each other but we can't communicate. One or both of us has ADHD and it's affecting everything."

**What we offer:** Couples coaching and counselling using EFT, Gottman, and Imago methods. Structured 4-phase process: Stabilise, Understand, Integrate, Sustain. Outcome: clearer communication, stronger emotional connection, conflict resolved not avoided.

### Individuals in transition

1:1 coaching and counselling

"I feel stuck. I know I'm capable of more but I don't know where to start or what direction to go. I've lost confidence."

**What we offer:** Individual coaching and counselling using Narrative Therapy, ACT, Solution-Focused, and CliftonStrengths. Personal goals, life transitions, self-management, confidence. Outcome: clarity, momentum, and a renewed sense of direction.

### Leaders and organisations

Executive coaching and team workshops

"My team is disengaged and we're not communicating well. I need to lead better but don't know where my blind spots are."

**What we offer:** Business and leadership coaching, CliftonStrengths-based team workshops, corporate programs for neurodiverse workplaces. Outcome: stronger leaders, aligned teams, measurable performance improvement.

## Channel Strategy

Where to focus marketing effort and what each channel does. Prioritised for a solo practitioner with limited time.

LinkedIn

**Primary channel.** Reaches ADHD adults, professionals, leaders, HR and L&D contacts in one place. Post 1–2 times per week using session-driven content angles (see Content Pipeline). Focus on relatable ADHD reframes, strengths insights, and proof points. Build the ThriveHQ audience and the corporate referral network from the same channel. Use **/social** to write posts.

Email list

**Nurture and convert.** Warm list of past clients, enquiries, and ThriveHQ prospects. Send one newsletter per month with a session insight or practical tip. Use a lead magnet (free Brain Pulse worksheet or SAFEty Pulse PDF) to grow the list. Welcome sequence for new subscribers should introduce the ThriveHQ framework. Use **/emails** to build sequences.

Website (SEO)

**Long-term discovery.** coach4u.com.au should have optimised service pages for: ADHD coaching Australia, strengths-based counselling, couples counselling online, executive coaching. Each page needs a clear CTA and social proof. Resources page for digital products. Blog posts from session content angles will build organic traffic over time. Use **/copywriting** for page copy.

Referrals

**Highest quality leads.** GP and specialist referrals for ADHD clients. Professional network — other coaches, therapists, psychologists. MHPN neurodiversity facilitation contacts. Build a one-page referral overview for each audience (GPs, therapists, HR managers). Past clients referring friends and family. This channel needs a formal process, not just word of mouth.

Speaking

**Authority and reach.** MHPN events, neurodiversity conferences, professional development panels, corporate lunch-and-learns. Each speaking engagement should be treated as a content event — record a short clip or write a follow-up post. Target 2–4 engagements per year in Year 1. Use **/marketing-ideas** to identify relevant venues.

Gumroad (digital products)

**Passive discovery and conversion.** Products embedded on coach4u.com.au. Buyers who download a Brain Pulse PDF are warm leads for ThriveHQ. Promote each product with a LinkedIn post focused on the problem it solves — not the product. Phase 1: Brain Pulse self-assessment. Phase 2: remaining PDFs and Starter Kit bundle.


## Content Strategy

Content is driven by what happens in ThriveHQ sessions. Each session generates content angles in client language — these become posts, emails, and blog articles. Use **/content-strategy** to plan further.

### Three content pillars

ADHD and the brain

Counterintuitive reframes, practical strategies, dopamine, executive function. What no-one tells you. Speaks to ThriveHQ prospects.

Strengths in action

How knowing your strengths changes what's possible. Real examples from individuals, couples, and teams. Positions the CliftonStrengths work.

Progress and proof

What sustained coaching actually looks like. Quiet wins, shifts in self-awareness, before-and-after moments. De-identified, paraphrased from sessions.

Every ThriveHQ session generates 5 content angles. After each session, run **/thrivehq-insights** on the transcript to produce a full marketing insights report with content ideas, client language, and proof points ready to use.

### Content rules

Content should reflect how clients actually talk about their experience — use the Language Bank in the Content Pipeline tab as the source of real language. Never use clinical terms. Never say "therapy." Focus on the problem the reader is experiencing right now, not the service being sold. One practical insight per piece is enough.


## 12-Month Marketing Plan

Four phases, building in sequence. Each phase has a clear goal and a set of concrete actions. Revenue and content build on each other.

Months 1–2 — Foundation

Get the basics live and start publishing.

- Set up Gumroad and connect to coach4u.com.au. List the Brain Pulse self-assessment PDF first — it is the most distinctive product and the easiest to describe.

- Publish the 10 content ideas from the May and March 2026 session insights. Track progress in the Content Pipeline tab.

Months 4–8 — Growth and Corporate

Add corporate revenue and build referral systems.

- Develop a team workshop offering with a one-page proposal template. Use **/marketing-ideas** to identify HR and L&D contacts. Target IAS and professional network first.
- Build a one-page referral brief for GPs and specialists — plain language, what to expect, how to refer, what makes Coach4U different from a psychology practice.
- Pitch for 2 speaking engagements — MHPN events, neurodiversity panels, or corporate lunch-and-learns. Prepare a short speaker bio and topic list.
- SEO-optimise service pages on coach4u.com.au. Target: "ADHD coaching Australia," "strengths-based counselling Sydney," "couples counselling online Australia." Use **/copywriting**.
- Launch ThriveHQ Starter Kit bundle on Gumroad at \$49 (Brain Pulse + Weekly Reset + Purpose doc). Write a launch email to the list.
- Add de-identified client proof points and success stories to coach4u.com.au — drawn from session insights reports, always paraphrased and anonymised.

Months 8–12 — Scale and Systemise

Consolidate revenue streams and plan Year 2.

- Conduct customer research — use session insights to build a proper persona document and voice-of-customer quote bank. Use **/customer-research**.
- Year 1 revenue review against targets by stream. Identify which channels produced leads and which need adjustment. Plan Year 2 with revised priorities.

## Brand Voice Guidelines

These rules apply to everything — posts, emails, website copy, product descriptions. They are non-negotiable and should be checked before anything is published.

### Do

- Warm, professional, clear
- Strengths-based — never problem-focused
- Australian English always
- Short paragraphs, easy to scan
- Practical over theoretical
- Use client language from the Language Bank
- Name the problem the reader is experiencing right now
- Close every email with "Thanks" — no other sign-off
- One insight per piece is enough

### Don't

- No exclamation marks
- No em dashes in prose (use commas or full stops)
- No clinical or therapy jargon
- Never call Coach4U a psychology practice
- Never use "therapy" or "therapeutic" in marketing copy
- No "Best regards," "Cheers," or "Kind regards" sign-offs
- No generic productivity advice ("just make a to-do list")
- No American English spelling
- No bundles of multiple changes in one piece
