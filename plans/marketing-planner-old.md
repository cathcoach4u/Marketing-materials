## Positioning

Helping teams and individuals thrive through strengths-based coaching, counselling, and collaboration.

Coach4U sits between traditional therapy and generic life coaching — offering more structure and measurable progress than counselling, and more psychological depth than most coaching services. The niche is the combination: evidence-based methods, delivered in a coaching style, with a strengths-first philosophy.

#### Integrative model

Evidence-based counselling techniques (EFT, Gottman, Narrative Therapy) delivered through a coaching lens — action-focused, not problem-focused.

#### Strengths-first

Every service leads with what clients are already good at. Challenges are framed as opportunities to use strengths in a new way.

#### ADHD and neurodiversity expertise

ThriveHQ is a flagship differentiator. Lived experience informs the approach. Extends to couples and organisational work with neurodiverse teams.

#### Continuous support ecosystem

Daily check-ins, group community, digital tools, and structured frameworks keep clients engaged between sessions — not just during them.

#### Structured pacing

Programs follow designed frameworks and stages (e.g. Stabilise, Understand, Integrate, Sustain for couples). Clients see tangible progress, not open-ended sessions.

#### Fully online, Australia-wide

All services delivered via Microsoft Teams. Accessible to clients anywhere, flexible to fit around busy schedules.

## Brand Voice Rules

Every piece of content — social, email, copy, video script — should follow these rules.

- ✓ Australian English in all written content
- ✓ Warm, grounded, professional — not clinical
- ✓ Short paragraphs, easy to scan
- ✓ Use real client language (paraphrased) — it resonates more than polished copy
- ✓ Lead with outcomes, not process
- ✓ Strengths-based framing — what's possible, not what's broken
- ✗ No exclamation marks
- ✗ No em dashes in prose — use commas or full stops
- ✗ No clinical or diagnostic language (disorder, syndrome, trauma)
- ✗ Never call Coach4U a psychology practice
- ✗ No therapy jargon (intervention, treatment, attachment wounds)
- ✗ No generic productivity advice that ignores how the brain actually works
