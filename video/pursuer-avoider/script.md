# The Dance — Pursuer and Avoider video script

> Journey video 1 for Your Relationship Coach. Square 1080x1080, around 95 seconds.
> Voice: Cath's cloned ElevenLabs voice (`V50HFUKIgwPl4QEG3try`). Couples accent `#be185d`.
> One TTS call per scene, silence-padded, timing JSON drives the scene cuts.
>
> Framing note: the same dynamic carries different names across the research. This script
> uses Cath's label (Pursuer and Avoider) as the spine and brings in Harville Hendrix's
> Imago language (the hailstorm and the turtle) as the picture that makes it stick.
> Sue Johnson's EFT calls it pursue–withdraw; Gottman tracks the withdrawing side as
> stonewalling. The video keeps it to the two framings clients hear in session.

## Scene spine

| # | Scene | On screen | Narration |
|---|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Hook | Title card: "The Dance" + subtitle "Pursuer and Avoider" | Have you noticed that the harder one of you tries to talk, the more the other one pulls away? You are not imagining it. It is a dance, and it has a name. |
| 2 | The Pursuer | Card: "The Pursuer — moves towards" + 3 dot points (wants to talk now, seeks reassurance, follows) | One of you moves towards. You want to talk about it now, fix it now, feel close again now. That is the pursuer. |
| 3 | The Avoider | Card: "The Avoider — moves away" + 3 dot points (goes quiet, needs space, waits for things to settle) | The other moves away. You go quiet, you need space, you wait for things to settle. That is the avoider. |
| 4 | Hailstorm and turtle | Split card: hailstorm icon / turtle icon, caption "Imago — Harville Hendrix" | Harville Hendrix calls this the hailstorm and the turtle. The hailstorm gets louder and bigger. The turtle pulls deeper into its shell. Different researchers, same dance. |
| 5 | The trap | Circular cycle diagram: pursue → feels like attack → withdraw → feels like abandonment → pursue | Here is the trap. The pursuing feels like a storm, so the turtle hides. The hiding feels like abandonment, so the storm grows. Each of you is protecting yourself, and each move sets off the other. |
| 6 | Underneath | Two fear cards: "If I stop reaching, we drift apart" / "If I engage, I will make it worse" | Underneath, the pursuer fears that if they stop reaching, you will drift apart. The avoider fears that if they engage, they will get it wrong and make it worse. Both fears make sense. Neither of you is the villain. |
| 7 | The reframe | Big single line: "It is not your characters. It is a cycle." | This is the part to hold onto. It is not your characters. It is a cycle. And a cycle can be changed. |
| 8 | Stepping out | 3 numbered steps: 1 Name it. 2 The turtle says when. 3 The storm softens. | So name it when you see it. I think we are in our dance right now. The turtle comes out enough to say when they will be back. And the storm softens enough to let them. |
| 9 | This week | Card: "Just notice the dance" | This week, you do not have to fix it. Just notice the dance, and notice which step is yours. Noticing is the first step out. |
| 10 | Sign-off | Animated word-by-word: "You are not opponents. You are partners learning the same dance." + Cath badge | You are not opponents. You are partners learning the same dance. I will see you in our next session. |

## Narration word count

About 240 words. With the lead-in, a beat between scenes and the tail, the runtime lands
around 95 seconds.

## Delivery notes

- Warm and unhurried. No exclamation marks anywhere in the narration.
- "I think we are in our dance right now" is spoken as the couple's line, slightly softer.
- The sign-off is personal — this video is shown to clients after a session, so "our next
  session" is correct, not "a session".
